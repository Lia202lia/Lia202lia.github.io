Academic identity

Who I am as an engineer

I was trained as a Robotics and Intelligent Systems Engineer in Latakia, Syria, where I learned to treat engineering as both a scientific discipline and a public responsibility. Working in an environment shaped by conflict and limited resources has taught me to design systems that are efficient, explainable, and mindful of the people who will rely on them.

My current interests lie in rehabilitation robotics, human–robot interaction, intelligent control, and computer vision. I am particularly drawn to problems where careful modeling, control, and perception can translate directly into improved quality of life for individuals recovering from injury or living with chronic motor impairments.

Beyond technical skills, I value perseverance, responsibility, service, and access to education. These values guide how I choose research problems, how I mentor younger students, and how I hope to contribute within an international graduate program.
