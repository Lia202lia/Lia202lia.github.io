About Me

Academic Identity & Research Philosophy

Educational Journey

My academic foundation is in Mechatronics Engineering, where I developed a robust understanding of mechanical design, electronic systems, and computational control. This interdisciplinary training ignited my passion for creating intelligent machines that can meaningfully interact with the physical world and assist humans. I am currently focused on advancing my expertise in Robotics and Intelligent Systems at the graduate level.

Research Motivation & Interests

I am driven by the potential of robotics to augment human capability, particularly in healthcare and rehabilitation. Witnessing the profound impact of limited access to advanced therapeutic technologies has solidified my commitment to developing effective, adaptable, and affordable robotic solutions. My research interests are consequently centered on:

  • Human-Robot Interaction: Creating intuitive and safe interfaces that allow seamless collaboration between users and robotic systems, prioritizing user intent and physical comfort.
  • Rehabilitation Robotics: Engineering exoskeletons and assistive devices that provide targeted, data-driven therapy to support motor recovery and improve quality of life.
  • Intelligent Control: Designing and implementing advanced control algorithms, including fuzzy logic and adaptive control, to manage uncertainty and enhance robotic autonomy in dynamic environments.
  • Computer Vision for Robotics: Employing vision-based techniques for environmental perception, object manipulation, and biomimetic control, such as mirroring human motion for robotic guidance.

Personal Values & Outlook

Beyond technical proficiency, I believe perseverance and meticulous problem-solving are essential for meaningful engineering. My work is guided by a strong sense of social responsibility—a belief that technology should strive to be equitable and serve broader humanitarian goals. I am particularly passionate about promoting access to education and mentorship in STEM fields, especially for aspiring engineers in regions facing adversity.

My international outlook is shaped by a desire to collaborate across cultures and disciplines. I seek to contribute to the global scientific community while remaining deeply connected to the specific needs and contexts from which my motivation springs. I approach my studies and research with a maturity that balances academic rigor with the authentic desire to create tangible, positive impact.
