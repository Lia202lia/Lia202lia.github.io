Research & Projects

Case studies in rehabilitation robotics, intelligent control, and human–robot interaction, with emphasis on quantitative performance and deployability.

Research overview

From human intention to controlled robotic assistance

My research focuses on how robotic systems can safely support and extend human capability, particularly in rehabilitation and assistive contexts. I work at the intersection of intelligent control, human–robot interaction, and perception, with the goal of producing systems that are both technically rigorous and clinically meaningful.

Hand Exoskeleton for Rehabilitation

Cable-driven hand exoskeleton for post-stroke rehabilitation with intelligent trajectory planning and supervision.

Problem statement

Many rehabilitation centers in low-resource settings lack access to consistent, high-intensity hand therapy, despite its importance for restoring motor function after stroke. The project addresses how a low-cost robotic exoskeleton can deliver repeatable, safe, and clinically relevant hand movements.

Methods & technical approach

  • Modeled hand kinematics and mapped joint motion to a cable-driven exoskeleton structure.
  • Implemented intelligent trajectory generation for flexion–extension movements under comfort and safety constraints.
  • Designed a supervisory controller combining sliding mode control with heuristic rules to handle spasticity and variable user response.
  • Deployed real-time control on embedded hardware with position and current sensing.

Quantitative results

  • Achieved trajectory tracking error below 5° for reference flexion–extension cycles at therapeutic speeds.
  • Maintained actuation forces within clinically acceptable comfort thresholds during pilot trials.
  • Reduced therapist time per session by enabling semi-autonomous repetitive exercises.

Fuzzy Logic Control of a Unicycle Mobile Robot

Intelligent path-following control for a unicycle robot under modeling uncertainty and external disturbances.

Problem statement

Classical controllers for mobile robots often degrade when friction, load changes, or sensor noise deviate from nominal models. This project explores fuzzy logic control as a robust alternative for trajectory tracking in unstructured environments.

Methods & technical approach

  • Modeled the unicycle kinematics and dynamics with nonholonomic constraints.
  • Designed a Mamdani-type fuzzy controller using linguistic rules for heading and velocity correction.
  • Tuned membership functions experimentally to balance responsiveness and smoothness.
  • Simulated performance across noisy and perturbed scenarios before hardware validation.

Quantitative results

  • Reduced steady-state lateral path error by approximately 40% compared with a baseline PID controller in simulation.
  • Improved heading convergence time while maintaining smooth linear velocity profiles.
  • Demonstrated robust tracking under injected sensor noise and variable ground friction models.

Stereo Vision for Mirrored Upper-Limb Motion

Stereo perception pipeline to estimate human arm pose and mirror it on a robotic device for intuitive control.

Problem statement

For many users, direct teleoperation of assistive robots is unintuitive and cognitively demanding. Mirroring human limb motion onto a robot can simplify control but requires reliable three-dimensional pose estimation.

Methods & technical approach

  • Configured and calibrated a stereo camera pair using standard calibration patterns.
  • Computed disparity maps and depth estimates for the upper-limb workspace.
  • Extracted keypoints and approximated joint angles for shoulder–elbow–wrist segments.
  • Mapped estimated pose to robot joint references under kinematic constraints.

Quantitative results

  • Achieved average endpoint position error on the order of a few centimeters within the calibrated workspace.
  • Maintained real-time pose estimation at interactive frame rates on modest hardware.
  • Enabled demonstration-based control where users could "teach" motions by example.

Robust Control for Assistive Robotic Platforms

Nonlinear and adaptive control strategies for assistive robots operating under uncertainty.

Problem statement

Assistive robots experience variable loads, unmodeled dynamics, and human interaction forces, which can destabilize naïve controllers. This project investigates robust control approaches that preserve stability and performance under such conditions.

Methods & technical approach

  • Formulated nonlinear models including bounded disturbances and parametric uncertainty.
  • Designed sliding mode and model reference adaptive controllers with Lyapunov-based stability guarantees.
  • Evaluated control laws in simulation across disturbance profiles that emulate human interaction.
  • Benchmarked tracking performance against classical PID in identical scenarios.

Quantitative results

  • Improved tracking performance under step disturbances, with reduced overshoot and faster settling.
  • Maintained stability margins under parameter variations within specified bounds.
  • Demonstrated potential to keep assistance within safe, predictable behavior envelopes.

Embedded Control for Low-Cost Robotic Systems

Implementation of real-time control loops for robotic prototypes using accessible hardware and toolchains.

Problem statement

Translating sophisticated control algorithms into low-cost embedded platforms can be challenging when computational and memory resources are constrained. This work focuses on implementing reliable control loops under such constraints.

Methods & technical approach

  • Prototyped control architectures using microcontrollers and single-board computers.
  • Implemented fixed-step control loops with careful timing and watchdog monitoring.
  • Optimized numeric operations and data logging to respect memory and CPU limits.
  • Validated loop stability under varying sampling intervals and sensor update rates.

Quantitative results

  • Maintained deterministic control loop execution within target cycle times.
  • Verified stability across representative load and timing variations.
  • Enabled reproducible experiments without high-end real-time hardware.

Educational Robotics for Early Talent Development

Design and delivery of robotics learning experiences for school and university students.

Problem statement

Many talented students in Syria and similar contexts have limited exposure to hands-on robotics and AI. This initiative explores how structured educational projects can build technical confidence and research readiness.

Methods & technical approach

  • Developed curricula covering basic electronics, microcontrollers, sensors, and actuation.
  • Guided students through project-based learning for competitions and exhibitions.
  • Introduced fundamental control and perception concepts using age-appropriate examples.
  • Encouraged documentation, reflection, and presentation of technical work.

Quantitative results

  • Supported teams that reached national-level robotics competitions.
  • Increased sustained participation in robotics activities over multiple cohorts.
  • Helped students build portfolios suitable for future academic applications.
