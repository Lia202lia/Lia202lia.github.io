Research & Projects

Hand Exoskeleton for Rehabilitation

January 2024 – August 2025

Designed and performed dynamic modeling of a self-aligning, lightweight, cable-driven hand exoskeleton optimized for safe human-robot interaction.

  • Implemented three control strategies for the nonlinear model, achieving less than 0.5° tracking error and a 20% reduction in settling time under disturbances.
  • Applied stereo vision-based hand pose mirroring, reducing mean joint position error from 22.4 mm to 15.4 mm with system latency under 200 ms.

Fuzzy Logic Based Control of a Unicycle Mobile Robot - Comparative Analysis

November 2023 – February 2024

Designed, implemented, and fine-tuned four Mamdani-type fuzzy controllers (P, PI, PD, PID-like) for controlling a unicycle mobile robot.

  • Achieved high accuracy in goal tracking: PID-like fuzzy control reached 0.001 m error with negligible oscillations, PI-like 0.05 m, PD-like 0.1 m.
  • Demonstrated robustness and smooth trajectory following through comparative analysis.
