Research Philosophy

My work is grounded in the belief that technology should extend human capabilities, not replace them. Each project begins with a human-centered design approach, considering not just technical feasibility but also accessibility, usability, and ethical implications.

I prioritize transparency and reproducibility, documenting methodologies and making code available to advance collective knowledge in robotics and intelligent systems.

Academic Journey

From the National Center for the Distinguished to Manara University's selective academic track, my education has been characterized by rigorous challenge and interdisciplinary exploration. This foundation enables me to approach complex problems from multiple perspectives.

My thesis work represents the culmination of this journey—applying advanced control theory to create tangible human benefit through rehabilitation technology.
