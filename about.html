About Me

Robotics engineer specializing in rehabilitation robotics, intelligent control, and human–robot interaction. My work focuses on designing systems that are technically rigorous, safe for close human use, and realistic for deployment in resource-constrained settings.

Academic Identity & Background

Robotics and Intelligent Systems Engineer trained in Syria, with experience in constrained-resource environments.

My Journey

I was trained as a Robotics and Intelligent Systems Engineer in Syria, where I learned to approach engineering with both rigor and humility. Working in an environment with constrained resources has pushed me to design systems that are efficient, frugal, and carefully justified.

Core Philosophy

"Engineering becomes meaningful when it restores human dignity and expands access to opportunity." This belief drives my focus on rehabilitation robotics and assistive technologies that serve underrepresented communities.

Current Focus

  • Human–robot interaction with emphasis on safety and transparency
  • Rehabilitation robotics for upper- and lower-limb motor recovery
  • Intelligent and fuzzy control for nonlinear systems
  • Computer vision for motion tracking and mirroring
