Rehabilitation Robotics

Hand Exoskeleton for Rehabilitation

Intelligent trajectory and control of a designed hand exoskeleton for motor recovery

Fig 1: CAD model showing self-alignment mechanism

Fig 2: Control architecture with vision feedback

Timeline: Jan 2024 – Aug 2025
Thesis Score: 96/100
Tools: SolidWorks, MATLAB/Simulink, ROS, Python

Project Overview

Designed and modeled a lightweight, cable-driven hand exoskeleton with self-alignment mechanisms for safe human-robot interaction. The system implements adaptive control strategies for patients with varying degrees of motor impairment.

Technical Approach

  • Dynamic modeling of 5-DOF finger mechanisms with tendon-driven actuation
  • Implementation of three control strategies (PID, adaptive, impedance control) for the nonlinear model
  • Stereo vision-based hand pose mirroring for intuitive control
  • Real-time sensor fusion using IMU and force sensors

Academic Contribution

This work demonstrates a cost-effective approach to adaptive rehabilitation robotics, combining mechanical innovation with intelligent control. The system's modular design allows for customization to individual patient needs, making such technology more accessible in resource-limited clinical settings.

Intelligent Control

Fuzzy Logic Based Control of a Unicycle Mobile Robot

Comparative analysis of Mamdani-type fuzzy controllers for non-holonomic systems

Timeline: Nov 2023 – Feb 2024
Platform: MATLAB/Simulink
Focus: Comparative Control Analysis

Research Objective

Designed, implemented, and fine-tuned four Mamdani-type fuzzy controllers (P, PI, PD, PID-like) to control a unicycle mobile robot, comparing performance against conventional control methods in trajectory tracking and disturbance rejection.

Methodology

  • Development of mathematical model for non-holonomic constraints
  • Design of fuzzy inference systems with triangular membership functions
  • Implementation of centroid defuzzification method
  • Comparative analysis under identical disturbance conditions

Performance Metrics

Controller Type Steady-State Error Overshoot Settling Time
PID-like Fuzzy 0.001 m Negligible 2.8 s
PI-like Fuzzy 0.05 m 2.1% 3.2 s
PD-like Fuzzy 0.1 m 1.8% 3.0 s
Conventional PID 0.08 m 4.5% 3.5 s

Key Findings

The PID-like fuzzy controller demonstrated superior performance in both accuracy and robustness, achieving near-zero steady-state error with minimal oscillation. Fuzzy controllers showed better disturbance rejection capabilities compared to conventional PID, particularly in handling model uncertainties and nonlinearities.
