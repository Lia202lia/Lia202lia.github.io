About Me

I am Tala Khaddour, a recent graduate in Robotics and Intelligent Systems Engineering from Manara University, where I pursued an advanced academic track as part of the Distinguished Student Program. My education has equipped me with a strong foundation in robotics, control systems, and artificial intelligence, culminating in an honors thesis on intelligent trajectory control for rehabilitation exoskeletons.

My motivation stems from a deep interest in bridging technology with human needs, particularly in areas where robotics can restore mobility and independence. Growing up in Syria, I witnessed the impact of limited access to advanced healthcare, which fueled my commitment to rehabilitation robotics.

My research interests include human-robot interaction, rehabilitation robotics, intelligent control strategies, and computer vision applications. I am particularly drawn to developing systems that ensure safe and adaptive interactions between humans and machines.

Guided by values of perseverance, social responsibility, and equitable access to education, I strive to contribute to global advancements in robotics while fostering international collaborations. My experiences have shaped an international outlook, emphasizing cross-cultural innovation in technology.
