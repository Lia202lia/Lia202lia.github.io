Academic Profile

Research Philosophy & Professional Development

Research Motivation

My work in robotics is driven by the conviction that intelligent systems should augment human capability, particularly in healthcare. Witnessing the limited access to advanced rehabilitation technologies inspired my focus on developing affordable, effective robotic solutions that bridge clinical needs with engineering innovation.

Academic Foundation

My training in Mechatronics Engineering provided rigorous grounding in mechanical design, control theory, and embedded systems. This interdisciplinary foundation enables me to approach robotics problems holistically, from mechanical implementation to intelligent control algorithms.

Research Philosophy

Human-Centered Design

Technology must serve human needs. All projects begin with user requirements and clinical applicability assessment.

Rigorous Methodology

Scientific approach with quantitative evaluation. Every design decision is supported by empirical evidence.

Accessible Innovation

Focus on solutions that can be implemented in resource-limited settings without compromising quality.

Collaborative Approach

Belief in interdisciplinary collaboration. Combine engineering with clinical insights for meaningful impact.

Technical Approach

I employ a systematic engineering methodology:

  1. Problem Analysis: Identify specific clinical or technical challenges
  2. Literature Review: Assess existing solutions and identify gaps
  3. System Design: Develop mechanical, electronic, and software architectures
  4. Implementation: Build and test prototypes with iterative refinement
  5. Evaluation: Quantitative performance assessment against metrics
  6. Documentation: Comprehensive reporting for reproducibility
