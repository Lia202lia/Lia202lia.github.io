About me

I am a Robotics and Intelligent Systems engineer committed to designing assistive technologies that respect human capabilities while extending them.

Academic background

My academic path in robotics and intelligent systems has been grounded in rigorous coursework in mechanics, control, embedded systems, and applied mathematics. Through this foundation, I learned to move fluently between modeling physical systems, implementing algorithms, and analyzing experimental data.

In my undergraduate studies, I focused on projects that required both careful hardware design and thoughtful algorithmic choices. I deliberately sought work that connected theoretical ideas in control and perception with tangible improvements in how robots support and collaborate with people.

Motivation for robotics

Robotics attracted me because it sits at the intersection of precision and empathy. The systems I design must satisfy strict technical constraints while also responding to the vulnerabilities and aspirations of the people who use them.

I am particularly motivated by contexts in which robotics can restore or enhance capabilities that illness, injury, or circumstance have limited. The design of such systems demands patience, humility, and a willingness to iterate in response to real users rather than idealized models.

Research interests

My research interests are centered on human–robot interaction, rehabilitation robotics, intelligent control, and computer vision. I am drawn to questions about how robots can interpret noisy, partial signals from humans and environments to make safe and helpful decisions.

In human–robot interaction and rehabilitation robotics, I am interested in assistive devices that adapt to individual users, capturing both biomechanical and behavioral patterns over time. Intelligent control and computer vision provide the tools to embed this adaptivity directly into the control loops that govern motion and feedback.

Values and outlook

Perseverance, responsibility, service, and access to education guide how I approach both research and collaboration. I view each project as a long-term commitment, from the initial literature review to the final documentation that enables others to build upon the work.

Growing up and studying in Syria has shaped an international outlook grounded in constraints and creativity. I am deeply aware that many communities face barriers to advanced healthcare, technology, and education, and I aspire to contribute to research that makes high-quality robotic assistance and learning opportunities more widely accessible.

Looking ahead

At the graduate level, I hope to join a research group where I can contribute to ongoing work in assistive and intelligent robotics while developing independent projects at the intersection of control, perception, and human factors. I am eager to collaborate with supervisors and peers who value careful experimentation, ethical reflection, and mentoring of the next generation of engineers.
