Academic Focus Areas

Rehabilitation Robotics

Designing and controlling exoskeletons and assistive devices for motor recovery, emphasizing human-centered design and clinical applicability.

Intelligent Control

Applying fuzzy logic, adaptive, and model-based control strategies to enhance the autonomy and performance of mobile and manipulator robots.

Computer Vision

Utilizing vision-based systems for pose estimation, environmental perception, and creating intuitive human-robot interfaces.

Human-Robot Interaction

Developing safe, natural, and effective interaction paradigms that leverage user intent for collaborative and assistive tasks.
