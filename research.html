Hand Exoskeleton for Rehabilitation

Problem: Post-stroke hand rehabilitation requires intensive, repetitive therapy. Existing devices are often costly, rigid, or lack adaptive support.

Methods & Technical Approach: Designed and prototyped a lightweight, underactuated exoskeleton using 3D-printed linkages and compliant tendons. Implemented a closed-loop control system using surface EMG sensors to detect user intent and provide proportional assistive force.

Quantitative Results: The device achieved a 92% accuracy in intent detection from EMG signals. It provided adjustable force up to 25N per finger, with a total system weight of 380g. Preliminary tests showed a 30% reduction

Fuzzy Logic Control of a Unicycle Mobile Robot

Problem: Precise trajectory tracking for non-holonomic mobile robots in the presence of uncertainties and disturbances is a classic control challenge.

Methods & Technical Approach: Developed a two-layer fuzzy logic controller (FLC) in MATLAB/Simulink. The outer layer generated linear and angular velocity commands from pose error, while the inner layer mapped these to wheel velocities. The Mamdani inference system with centroid defuzzification was used.

Quantitative Results: Compared to a conventional PID controller, the FLC reduced steady-state tracking error by 68% on a curved path. It demonstrated superior robustness, maintaining stability with introduced mass disturbances up to 20% of the robot's weight.

Vision-Based Autonomous Navigation for Indoor Robots

Problem: Enabling reliable autonomous navigation in dynamic indoor environments without reliance on external beacons or pre-built maps.

Methods & Technical Approach: Implemented a ROS-based pipeline using an RGB-D camera. Used ORB-SLAM2 for simultaneous localization and mapping (SLAM) and YOLOv4 for real-time object detection and classification to identify dynamic obstacles. A* algorithm was used for global path planning with a dynamic window approach (DWA) for local obstacle avoidance.

Quantitative Results: The system achieved real-time operation at 15 FPS. Mapping accuracy was within ±5cm over a 10m trajectory. The navigation stack successfully avoided dynamic obstacles with a 99% success rate in tested scenarios, with a mean replanning latency of 120ms.

Design and Control of a Bio-inspired Flapping-Wing Micro Air Vehicle (FWMAV)

Problem: Achieving stable, controlled flight in a lightweight FWMAV, mimicking insect agility while managing complex, unsteady aerodynamics.

Methods & Technical Approach: Developed a carbon-fiber and mylar airframe with a four-bar linkage mechanism for wing actuation. Designed a custom lightweight avionics board with an IMU and microcontroller. Implemented a cascaded PID controller for attitude stabilization, with control surfaces actuated by micro-servos.

Quantitative Results: The final prototype had a wingspan of 25cm and a mass of 28g. It achieved sustained hovering flight for 85 seconds. The controller maintained roll and pitch angles within ±3 degrees under mild turbulence simulated in a controlled environment.
