Robotics & Intelligent Systems Engineer

"Engineering becomes meaningful when it restores human dignity and expands access to opportunity."

I am a robotics engineer specializing in rehabilitation robotics, intelligent control, and human–robot interaction. My work focuses on designing systems that are technically rigorous, safe for close human use, and realistic for deployment in resource-constrained settings.

This website is submitted as part of my international scholarship applications and reflects my preparation for graduate-level research in robotics and AI.

Research focus

From theoretical control to human-centered deployment

Rehabilitation robotics

Design and control of hand exoskeletons and assistive devices, with emphasis on safety, comfort, and clinically relevant performance metrics.

Intelligent control

Fuzzy and nonlinear control strategies for complex robotic systems, targeting robust trajectory tracking and disturbance rejection.

Perception & HRI

Stereo vision and pose estimation for mirroring human motion, enabling intuitive, transparent interaction between users and robotic systems.
