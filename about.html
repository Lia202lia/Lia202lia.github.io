Research Philosophy

An integrated approach to intelligent systems design

Human-Centered Design

I believe technology should adapt to human needs, not the reverse. My projects begin with understanding user constraints, physical capabilities, and psychological comfort, ensuring solutions are both effective and accessible.

Interdisciplinary Integration

Breakthroughs occur at disciplinary boundaries. I actively combine control theory, mechanical design, computer vision, and cognitive science, believing that the most innovative solutions emerge from cross-disciplinary thinking.

Ethical Implementation

From data privacy in biometric sensing to safety in physical human-robot interaction, ethical considerations are integral to my design process, not an afterthought. Responsible innovation guides every technical decision.

Guiding Principles

Perseverance

Engineering complex systems requires resilience in the face of iterative failure. I view each challenge as a necessary step toward a robust, well-tested solution.

Social Responsibility

I believe engineers bear responsibility for the societal impact of their work. My focus on rehabilitation robotics stems from this commitment to serving those with limited access to advanced care.

Global Perspective

My experience has fostered appreciation for cross-cultural collaboration. I am committed to contributing to global scientific discourse while addressing challenges that transcend borders.

Knowledge Sharing

I actively mentor younger students, believing that empowering others amplifies impact. Education should be accessible, and knowledge should be shared to build collective capability.

Problem-Solving Approach

1

Define & Understand

Begin with thorough problem analysis: literature review, stakeholder considerations, and constraint identification. What's the real need behind the technical challenge?

2

Model & Simulate

Develop mathematical models and run simulations before physical implementation. This conserves resources and reveals hidden complexities early in the design process.

3

Prototype & Iterate

Build functional prototypes using rapid fabrication methods. Each iteration incorporates testing feedback, with quantitative metrics driving design decisions.

4

Validate & Document

Conduct rigorous validation against predefined success criteria. Document thoroughly—results, limitations, and lessons learned—for academic contribution and reproducibility.
