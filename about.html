My Academic Journey

From robotics enthusiast to innovative researcher focused on human-centered technology

Engineering with Purpose

My journey in robotics began not just with an interest in technology, but with a conviction that engineering should serve human needs. Growing up in Syria, I witnessed firsthand how technology could bridge gaps in access and opportunity, particularly in healthcare and education.

This perspective shaped my academic path at Manara University, where I pursued Robotics and Intelligent Systems Engineering through the Distinguished Student Program. Here, I didn't just learn engineering principles—I learned to apply them to real human challenges.

My thesis on intelligent trajectory control for a hand exoskeleton represents the convergence of my technical skills and my commitment to human-centered design. By achieving <0.5° tracking error and reducing settling time by 20%, I demonstrated that precision engineering can directly translate to more effective rehabilitation tools.

Beyond the Lab

Academic excellence is only one dimension of my development. Through volunteering with organizations like Acquaint and Saiar Team, I've engaged with communities and understood the social context of technological solutions. As a robotics coach and trainer, I've learned that innovation is as much about empowering others as it is about technical breakthroughs.

My participation in international programs like the Girls in AI Hackathon and InnoSync Conference has given me a global perspective on technology's potential and challenges. These experiences have reinforced my belief that diverse teams create more innovative and equitable solutions.

Core Principles

Human-Centered Design

Technology should extend human capabilities, not replace them. Every system I design prioritizes intuitive interaction and accessibility.

Rigorous Inquiry

From comparative analysis of fuzzy controllers to precise trajectory tracking, my work is grounded in methodical research and validation.

Global Perspective

Innovation thrives at the intersection of diverse perspectives. I actively seek cross-cultural collaboration and international exchange.

Mentorship & Education

Knowledge gains value when shared. I'm committed to coaching emerging engineers and making robotics education more accessible.

Looking Forward

As I apply for prestigious scholarships and graduate programs, I seek to deepen my research in assistive robotics and human-robot interaction. I'm particularly interested in adaptive control systems that can respond to individual user needs in real-time.

My goal is to contribute to a future where robotic systems seamlessly integrate into therapeutic and assistive contexts, enhancing human agency rather than replacing it. I believe the most profound engineering solutions are those that become invisible—woven so seamlessly into human experience that they feel like natural extensions of our capabilities.
