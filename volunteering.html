Service Philosophy

Knowledge as Empowerment

I believe technical education should be accessible to all, particularly to underrepresented groups in STEM. My mentoring focuses not only on technical skills but also on building confidence and problem-solving mindset.

Collaborative Growth

Service is most effective when it creates sustainable impact. I emphasize mentorship structures where students eventually become mentors themselves, creating a cycle of knowledge sharing.

Local Context, Global Perspective

While addressing local educational needs, I connect students to global scientific discourse. This dual focus helps build both practical skills and broader scientific literacy.

Impact & Reflection

From Student to Mentor

"Watching students I mentored go on to develop their own projects and, in turn, mentor others has been particularly rewarding. This multiplier effect is where service creates lasting impact."
