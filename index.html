Tala Khaddour

Robotics & Intelligent Systems Engineer

I design human-centered robotic systems that combine intelligent control, computer vision, and principled engineering to restore and enhance motor function, particularly for people in under-resourced and post-conflict settings.

“Engineering becomes meaningful when it restores human dignity and expands access to opportunity.”

This website is submitted as part of my international scholarship applications.

Research directions

  • Rehabilitation and assistive robotics
  • Human–robot interaction and safety-aware control
  • Intelligent and fuzzy control of nonlinear systems
  • Stereo vision and perception for robotic manipulation

Solid foundations

Through the Robotics and Intelligent Systems Engineering program at Manara University, I completed rigorous coursework in robotics, intelligent control, computer vision, and embedded systems, culminating in an honors-level thesis on hand exoskeleton control.

Research orientation

My thesis and subsequent projects focus on quantitative performance, model-based design, and reproducible experimentation, which align closely with the expectations of competitive graduate programs in robotics and AI.

Global perspective

Growing up and studying in Syria, I have learned to treat engineering as a tool for resilience and reconstruction, and I am committed to contributing to international research communities while keeping societal impact at the center.
