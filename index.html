From modeling to impact

In my hand exoskeleton thesis, I designed a self-aligning, cable-driven device and implemented three intelligent controllers, reducing tracking error to less than 0.5° and shortening settling time under disturbances by around 20%.

Control & intelligence

Through fuzzy control of a unicycle mobile robot, I tuned Mamdani-type controllers that achieved goal-tracking errors down to 0.001 m with smooth, low-oscillation trajectories suitable for shared human–robot spaces.

Vision & embodiment

By integrating stereo vision–based hand pose mirroring into the exoskeleton system, I reduced mean joint position error from 22.4 mm to 15.4 mm while maintaining system latency under 200 ms, strengthening real-time human–robot coupling.
