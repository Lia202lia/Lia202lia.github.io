Volunteering & Academic Service

Teaching, mentoring, and academic service activities that support young learners, promote access to education, and strengthen the robotics community.

Commitment to service

Education, ethics, and long-term impact

Volunteering and academic service are central to how I understand engineering. Alongside my technical work, I dedicate time to mentoring students, supporting science programs, and helping young learners see themselves as future engineers and researchers.

Service areas

Where I try to make a difference

Robotics education & mentoring

Designing and delivering robotics workshops that introduce students to sensors, control, and problem-solving through hands-on projects.

Science Olympiad support

Supporting students preparing for STEM competitions by guiding practice, reviewing solutions, and encouraging systematic thinking.

Distinction and Creativity Agency

Engaging with programs that identify and nurture highly motivated students, reinforcing perseverance and responsibility in academic work.

Youth development

Contributing to initiatives that help young people in Syria build confidence, communication skills, and a sense of agency in their future.

Humanitarian mindset

Approaching engineering and education as tools for restoring dignity, widening access, and supporting communities affected by crisis.

Selected roles

Examples of sustained engagement

Robotics & Programming Trainer

Syrian Computer Society — Latakia & Jableh

As a trainer, I worked with school and university students to introduce robotics concepts, embedded programming, and basic AI. The goal was not only to complete projects, but to build students' confidence in reading documentation, troubleshooting, and presenting their work.

  • Delivered structured sessions on microcontrollers, sensors, and actuators.
  • Guided students through the full cycle from idea to working prototype.
  • Encouraged teamwork, reflection, and peer feedback as core habits.

Support for Science Competitions

Science Olympiad and STEM-focused initiatives

I contributed to the preparation of students for science and technology competitions, focusing on problem interpretation, solution structure, and strategies for managing time and stress in high-stakes environments.

  • Helped students break complex problems into manageable sub-tasks.
  • Reviewed solutions and highlighted rigorous reasoning and clarity.
  • Reinforced ethics, collaboration, and respect for peers and judges.

Engagement with Distinction and Creativity Agency

Supporting pathways for high-potential students

Through scholarship and distinction programs, I interacted with students who, like me, were selected for their academic potential. These experiences strengthened my commitment to keeping academic spaces inclusive, demanding, and supportive.

  • Shared experiences on navigating demanding curricula and research expectations.
  • Discussed long-term planning for scholarships and graduate study.
  • Emphasized responsibility and service as parts of academic excellence.

Youth & Community Activities

Local youth-focused initiatives

Outside formal institutions, I participate in activities that give young people space to discuss their goals, challenges, and hopes. These conversations inform how I think about the social responsibility of robotics and AI.

  • Participated in dialogue-based workshops on education and careers.
  • Encouraged participants to see engineering as a tool for service.
  • Connected interested students with available programs and resources.

Values in practice

How volunteering shapes my engineering work

Perseverance

Working with students who persist despite limited resources continually reminds me to approach technical obstacles with patience and determination.

Responsibility

Mentoring younger learners reinforces my sense of duty to communicate clearly, act ethically, and model careful engineering practice.

Service & access

Volunteering keeps me focused on the role of robotics and intelligent systems in widening access to education, healthcare, and opportunity.
