Academic Service & Social Impact

Leadership, mentoring, and commitment to community.

Academic Service & Committees

I have actively contributed to the academic community through formal roles that require diligence, judgment, and a commitment to scholarly standards.

  • Distinction and Creativity Agency: Served as a member, participating in the evaluation and selection of exceptional student projects and research initiatives, fostering a culture of innovation.
  • Faculty Student Committee: Acted as a representative, facilitating communication between the student body and faculty on curriculum and academic policy matters.
  • Graduation Project Jury: Participated as a jury member for final-year project defenses, providing technical feedback and assessment.

Science Olympiad Support & Mentoring

I am passionate about inspiring the next generation of scientists and engineers, particularly young women in STEM.

  • National Science Olympiad Trainer: Prepared and coached student teams for national robotics and programming competitions. My teams achieved multiple top-three finishes.
  • Robotics Workshop Instructor: Designed and delivered hands-on workshops on introductory robotics, sensor integration, and basic programming for high school students.
  • University Club Mentor: Provided ongoing technical guidance and project oversight for the university's robotics club, assisting with conceptual design and problem-solving.

Youth Development & Humanitarian Involvement

My understanding of engineering's purpose is rooted in its capacity to serve people and improve lives.

  • Youth Development Center Volunteer: Contributed to programs focused on soft skills development, career guidance, and educational support for underserved youth.
  • Educational Access Initiatives: Participated in local drives to provide textbooks and learning materials to students in need, believing firmly in education as a fundamental right.
  • Community Technical Support: Offered pro-bono technical assistance to local NGOs and community centers, repairing equipment and setting up basic digital infrastructure.

Reflection on Service

“Leadership in engineering is not solely about technical prowess; it is about leveraging one's skills to elevate others, to demystify complex subjects, and to ensure that the benefits of technology are directed toward inclusive and humanitarian ends. My service activities are an integral part of my professional identity, reinforcing the ethical framework within which I conduct my research.”
