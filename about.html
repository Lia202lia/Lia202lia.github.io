Tala Khaddour

Robotics & Intelligent Systems Engineer

A passionate robotics engineer dedicated to creating intelligent systems that enhance human capabilities, with particular focus on affordable rehabilitation technologies for global healthcare challenges.

Early Academic Foundation

My journey in robotics began at the National Center for the Distinguished, where I ranked 3rd among 75 selected students nationwide. This early exposure to rigorous academic training cultivated my analytical thinking and passion for engineering innovation.

Robotics Engineering Education

At Manara University, I pursued a Bachelor's in Robotics and Intelligent Systems Engineering through the Distinguished Student Program. The curriculum emphasized advanced coursework in control systems, artificial intelligence, and human-robot interaction, preparing me for graduate-level research.

Research Focus: Rehabilitation Robotics

My honors thesis on "Intelligent Trajectory and Control of a Designed Hand Exoskeleton for Rehabilitation" represents my commitment to bridging engineering excellence with human welfare. This work addresses the critical need for affordable assistive devices in regions with limited healthcare resources.

Global Perspective & Aspirations

Through international competitions like the World Robot Olympiad and participation in global initiatives like Girls in AI, I've developed a cross-cultural understanding of technology's role in society. I aim to contribute to international research collaborations that advance accessible rehabilitation technologies worldwide.

Research Philosophy

Guiding principles that shape my approach to robotics engineering

Human-Centered Design

Technology should serve humanity. Every robotic system I design prioritizes accessibility, usability, and direct positive impact on human lives, particularly in healthcare applications.

Ethical Innovation

Responsible engineering requires considering the social, economic, and ethical implications of technological advancement, ensuring solutions are sustainable and equitable.

Collaborative Excellence

Breakthroughs happen at the intersection of disciplines. I value cross-cultural and interdisciplinary collaboration as essential for solving complex global challenges.

Global Perspective

Engineering solutions must consider diverse contexts. I'm committed to developing technologies that are adaptable to different cultural and economic environments worldwide.
