Research

Rehabilitation robotics, intelligent control, and human–robot interaction with a focus on clinically meaningful, deployable systems.

Research overview

From exoskeletons to intelligent supervision of human–robot collaboration

My research interests center on designing and controlling robotic systems that operate safely in close proximity to humans, particularly in rehabilitation contexts. This includes hand exoskeletons, intelligent control strategies, and perception pipelines that allow robots to interpret and respond to human motion.

Featured projects

Selected work demonstrating technical depth and human-centered design

Hand Exoskeleton for Rehabilitation

Rehabilitation robotics · Intelligent control

Design and control of a cable-driven hand exoskeleton aimed at supporting post-stroke rehabilitation. The system integrates trajectory planning, intelligent supervision, and safety-aware actuation for repetitive training tasks.

Role
Lead designer & control engineer
Methods
SMC, fuzzy logic, inverse kinematics, embedded control
Status
Thesis project – completed

Stereo Vision for Mirrored Motion

Perception · Human–robot interaction

Development of a stereo camera pipeline to estimate upper-limb pose and mirror human motion on a robotic device, enabling intuitive, demonstration-based control of assistive robots.

Role
Computer vision engineer
Methods
Calibration, depth estimation, kinematic mapping
Status
Prototype – ongoing refinement

Robust Control of Assistive Robots

Nonlinear control · Robustness

Exploration of robust and adaptive control techniques for assistive robotic platforms operating under uncertainty, emphasizing trajectory tracking and disturbance rejection in human-centered environments.

Role
Control design & simulation
Methods
SMC, MRAC, Lyapunov-based analysis
Status
Simulation studies with hardware roadmap

Thematic areas

How these projects connect

Human-centered design

Emphasis on comfort, safety, and clinical usability, ensuring that prototypes can realistically transition to practice.

Intelligent supervision

Controllers that combine model-based reasoning with heuristic or data-driven layers to manage uncertainty and variability.

Accessible deployment

Solutions tailored for resource-constrained settings, balancing performance with hardware availability and maintainability.
