Robotics & Intelligent Systems Engineer

Tala Khaddour

I design and study intelligent robotic systems that can sense, interpret, and respond to human needs with precision, safety, and dignity in rehabilitation and assistive contexts.

This website is submitted as part of my international scholarship applications.

Research focus

  • Human–robot interaction
  • Rehabilitation robotics
  • Intelligent control
  • Computer vision for embodied systems
View academic CV Explore research projects

Graduate-level readiness

My work integrates rigorous modeling, control theory, and experimental validation to build robotic systems that perform reliably under real-world uncertainty and human variability.

Interdisciplinary outlook

I draw on mechanical design, embedded systems, and machine learning to connect low-level actuation with high-level perception and decision-making in assistive robots.

Ethical motivation

My long-term goal is to expand access to high-quality rehabilitation and education by creating affordable, evidence-based robotic technologies in under-resourced settings.

Selected research directions

  • Design of hand exoskeletons for post-stroke rehabilitation with adaptive, patient-specific control.
  • Fuzzy and nonlinear control of underactuated mobile robots for safe navigation in constrained spaces.
  • Vision-based intent recognition to anticipate user actions and reduce cognitive load during assistance.
  • Evaluation protocols that combine quantitative performance metrics with user-centered measures of trust and comfort.

What I bring to a lab

  • Experience bridging simulation and hardware through careful calibration, instrumentation, and iterative prototyping.
  • Commitment to transparent, well-documented experimentation and reproducible results.
  • Capacity to mentor younger students and contribute to a collaborative, inclusive research culture.
