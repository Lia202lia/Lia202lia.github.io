About Me

I am a Robotics and Intelligent Systems Engineer who sees research not only as a technical pursuit, but as a responsibility to expand access, restore function, and create dignified futures.

Academic Identity

I was trained as a Robotics and Intelligent Systems Engineer in Syria, where I learned to approach engineering with both rigor and humility. Working in an environment with constrained resources has pushed me to design systems that are efficient, frugal, and carefully justified.

My current interests lie at the intersection of human–robot interaction, rehabilitation robotics, and intelligent control, with a particular focus on applications that serve people who are often excluded from advanced technologies.
